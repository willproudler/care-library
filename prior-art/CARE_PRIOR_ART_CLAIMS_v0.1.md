# CARE Prior Art Claims v0.1

Initial public date: 11 July 2026

Author: William Proudler

Version: v0.1.0

Release date: 15 July 2026

## Purpose

This document lists the core CARE concepts and methods being asserted as prior art.

These claims are written to make the CARE architecture legible as an existing method, not merely as a speculative idea.

## Prior art claim 1: recursive source-grounded canon architecture

CARE is a recursive architecture for processing texts into structured interpretive units at multiple scales.

The basic movement is:

```text
SOURCE
→ RAW
→ RAW PASSAGES
→ CH
→ CL
→ MC
→ WB
→ CB / XS / SC / OV
```

## Prior art claim 2: passage-first ingestion

In CARE v0.1 and later, passage generation occurs at the point of RAW ingestion.

This ensures that interpretive outputs can be tied back to source passages.

## Prior art claim 3: interpretive unit hierarchy

CARE defines an interpretive unit hierarchy:

- RAW: cleaned source unit;
- PASSAGE: source-grounded passage unit;
- CH: chapter-level interpretive unit;
- CL: cluster-level synthesis;
- MC: metacluster synthesis;
- WB: whole-book synthesis;
- CB: corpus synthesis;
- XS: cross-lineage synthesis;
- SC: strange-child synthesis;
- OV: overlay synthesis.

## Prior art claim 4: source ancestry inheritance

Higher-level CARE units may inherit source ancestry from lower-level units.

A WholeBook claim may therefore be traced backward through the chain of synthesis.

## Prior art claim 5: forkable canon

CARE proposes the canon as a forkable, inspectable, revisable structure.

A canon should not be treated as a closed institutional list. It can be treated as a versioned, comparative, source-grounded, open structure.

## Prior art claim 6: comparative apophatic reading

CARE can compare not only what texts say, but what they cannot stabilise, what they exclude, what they circle around, and what returns as contradiction or pressure.

## Prior art claim 7: corpus-level synthesis

Multiple WholeBook units may be compared or synthesised into corpus-level units.

This allows CARE to map recurring axioms, contradictions, exclusions, and structures across books.

## Prior art claim 8: cross-lineage synthesis

CARE can compare books across authors, periods, disciplines, traditions, and conceptual lineages.

Examples include:

- Nietzsche / Spinoza / Deleuze;
- Marx / Baudrillard / Bataille;
- Genesis / Revelation / CCRU;
- Kant / Hegel / Land;
- Plato / Descartes / Kant.

## Prior art claim 9: strange-child synthesis

CARE may generate experimental synthesis units from incompatible or distant source lineages.

These are not presented as summaries of existing books, but as new conceptual offspring produced from traceable parent structures.

## Prior art claim 10: commons-oriented anti-enclosure structure

CARE is intended as a commons protocol rather than a closed product.

The name, provenance, and compatibility structure should prevent misrepresentation or capture by closed systems while allowing legitimate study, forking, correction, and extension.
