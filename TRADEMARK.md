# CARE Library Trademark Policy

## Current status

`CARE Library` is the subject of pending UK trademark application `UK00004414316`.

- **Filing date:** 10 July 2026
- **Current status:** Examination
- **Classes:** 9, 41 and 42
- **Status last checked:** 14 July 2026

The official status should be verified through the UK Intellectual Property Office register before relying on this notice.

The application is pending and is not a registration. The registered trademark symbol `®` must not be used unless registration is granted.

`CARE Machine` is an associated project name. This notice does not represent that `CARE Machine` is included in application `UK00004414316` or separately registered.

## Purpose

Trademark protection is intended to preserve the identity, provenance and integrity of the open CARE commons.

It is not intended to prevent legitimate study, citation, criticism, teaching, compatible implementation, forking, or non-misleading description of CARE-derived work.

## Uses that do not require separate permission

Subject to applicable law, the following uses are generally acceptable:

- accurate reference to CARE Library or its published documents;
- citation, criticism, scholarship, reporting or teaching;
- truthful statements that a project implements or is compatible with published CARE specifications;
- forks and adaptations distributed under a clearly distinct project name;
- links to the canonical public repository;
- statements that accurately identify the historical origin of CARE material.

These uses must not imply endorsement, sponsorship, governance, certification or official status.

## Uses requiring prior permission

Prior written permission should be obtained before:

- using `CARE Library` as the primary name of another project, service, organisation or publication;
- using a confusingly similar name, logo or visual identity;
- registering a domain, package, account, application or organisation name likely to appear official;
- describing a fork or implementation as the canonical or official CARE Library;
- using CARE branding in advertising, fundraising, merchandise or commercial promotion;
- claiming certification, approval or endorsement by the CARE Library project.

## Forks and compatible implementations

Forks may use the licensed documentation according to its copyright licence, but must use distinguishable branding.

A fork or compatible implementation should display a notice substantially similar to:

> This is an independent project based on published CARE materials. It is not the official CARE Library and is not endorsed or governed by the CARE Library project.

Compatibility claims must identify the specification or release version being implemented.

## No effect on copyright licensing

The CC BY-SA, future AGPL and future ODbL licensing statements do not grant trademark rights.

Permission to copy, modify or distribute licensed material does not authorize misleading use of the CARE Library name or identity.

## Monitoring checklist

The project should:

- review the official UKIPO register regularly while the application is under examination;
- retain and respond to official correspondence by its stated deadlines;
- monitor the UK trademark journal if the application is published;
- diarise the opposition period and any extension;
- search periodically for confusingly similar marks in classes 9, 41 and 42;
- monitor major code hosts, package registries, domains, application stores and social platforms;
- preserve dated public evidence of genuine CARE Library use;
- maintain a record of relevant names, URLs, dates, territories and goods or services;
- keep domain and repository accounts secured with strong recovery controls and two-factor authentication;
- if registration is granted, record the registration date and renewal deadline.

## Potential misuse

Suspected misuse should be assessed for:

- similarity of the name or branding;
- similarity of the relevant goods or services;
- likelihood of confusion;
- claims of endorsement or official status;
- territory and audience;
- whether the use is descriptive, critical, scholarly or otherwise lawful.

Preserve public evidence before taking action. Do not send legal threats or public accusations without appropriate review or professional advice.

## Private records

Trademark filing documents, correspondence, receipts, payment information, contact details, domain receipts and enforcement records must remain private.

Only non-sensitive application facts and public-register information should appear in the repository.
